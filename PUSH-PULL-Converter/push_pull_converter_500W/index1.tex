\section{Overview Of Power Electronic}
\subsection{Introduction to Push-Pull Converter} 
The push-pull converter is an efficient, isolated DC-DC topology that uses a transformer to transfer energy while providing galvanic isolation, enhancing safety, noise reduction, and voltage scaling. It operates by alternately switching two transistors (e.g., MOSFETs) to drive current through the transformer’s primary winding, generating AC that is rectified to DC on the secondary side. Ideal for medium-to-high power applications like telecom systems, renewable energy inverters, and industrial power supplies, it balances high efficiency, robust performance, and effective transformer utilization. Its minimized switching losses, compact design, and reliability make it a cornerstone of modern power electronics.
\subsubsection*{Key Features of the Push-Pull Converter}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Galvanic Isolation}: Achieved through a transformer, ensuring safety and preventing direct electrical contact between input and output stages.
    \item \textbf{High Efficiency}: Symmetrical switching and minimal conduction losses enable operation in medium-to-high power ranges (tens to hundreds of watts).
    \item \textbf{Bidirectional Energy Transfer}: The push-pull action allows efficient energy flow in both directions through the transformer.
    \item \textbf{Transformer Utilization}: Balanced operation reduces core saturation risks, enabling compact and optimized transformer designs.
    \item \textbf{Wide Input/Output Voltage Range}: Voltage scaling is easily adjustable via the transformer turns ratio.
    \item \textbf{Robust Performance}: Isolation and resilience to voltage spikes make it suitable for harsh environments.
    \item \textbf{Control Flexibility}: PWM controllers manage switching timing, ensuring precise output regulation and fault protection.
\end{itemize}
